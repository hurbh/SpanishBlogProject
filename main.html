Spanish dish: Patatas bravas (from Spain!)

Posted on May 2, 2025 by Hasnat Anwar

Patatas bravas (also known as patatas a la brava or papas bravas) is a spicy potato dish originating from Spain. You make it by frying cubes of wide white potatoes and serving them with a spicy "brava" sauce, which usually consists of smoked paprika, olive oil, and a thickening agent like corn starch. Source

Patatas Bravas

Patatas bravas is a popular tapas recipe that exists in most Spanish homes and restaurants. It is simple enough to be made in various ways, making it versatile and a favorite of all. The potatoes are usually crunchy outside, airy inside, and covered in a spicy, smoky sauce (i.e. "Bravas" sauce)that gives it an extra layer of flavor. This dish is perfect as a snack, appetizer, or side dish to complement any meal. Source

Fun Fact: Did You Know?

Fun Fact: Did You Know? Patatas Bravas is traditionally served as a "tapa," a tiny snack or appetizer, but it became so popular that it's now a staple on most Spanish restaurant menus. In fact, most bars in Spain have unofficial "Patatas Bravas contests" to see who can make the best version of this favorite dish, usually with their own secret recipe for the brava sauce! Source
